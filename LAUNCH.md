# BarberQueue — Launch Playbook & Sales Guide

**Last updated:** 2026-05-31
**Market:** Toronto independent barbershops
**Language:** English (used in all in-person visits)

---

## Product Readiness Summary

The core product works: booking, automations, multi-barber, SMS, Stripe deposits, waitlist, branding. A barbershop using it today has a better tool than most competitors at this price. Three things to be aware of before pitching:

- **No automated billing yet** — collect payment manually via e-transfer or cash for first clients. Track renewals in a spreadsheet or Notion.
- **No onboarding wizard** — you are the onboarding. Leave the setup checklist (below) after every visit.
- **Stripe setup needed for deposits** — walk them through it in person. Takes 10 minutes.

**Launch pricing:** $15/month for first clients (launch discount from $19.99). Multi-barber shops: $25/month (from $29.99). No contracts. Cancel anytime.

## Prospecting Strategy — Find the Right Shops First

The highest-conversion targets are barbershops that **have no online booking**. They already feel the pain — you don't need to convince them the problem exists.

**How to prospect before a visit:**
Open Google Maps, search "barbershop" in your target neighborhood, and look for:

| Signal | What it means | Priority |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Google profile, no "Book" button | No booking tool at all | ✅ Highest |
| Few or no reviews, no website | Established but not digital | ✅ Highest |
| Instagram link only, no booking | Tried something but incomplete | 🟡 High |
| Has "Book" button on Maps | Already using a tool | ❌ Harder sell |

**The Google Maps angle — use this in person:**
> *"I looked up your shop on Google Maps before coming in. You don't have a booking link there — your clients have to call. I can set that up for you right now, while I'm here. It takes two minutes."*

This creates immediate visible value on the first visit. You fix a real problem while they watch.

**Best neighborhoods to start (high barbershop density):**
- **Eglinton West** (Little Jamaica) — many independent shops, loyal clientele
- **Scarborough** (Kennedy Rd, Lawrence Ave) — high density, low digitization
- **Jane & Finch** — established shops, owners open to growth tools
- **Kensington / Dundas West** — younger owners, more tech-receptive

Start in one neighborhood. Get 3–5 referrals before moving to the next. Word travels fast in these communities.

## The Sales Script

### Opening (first 30 seconds)

Walk in during a slow moment — mid-morning or early afternoon on weekdays. Never during rush hour.

> *"Hey, how's it going? My name is [name], I'm the founder of BarberQueue — it's a booking and management software built specifically for barbershops here in Toronto. Do you have two minutes? I want to show you something on my phone."*

Key points:
- Say **founder**, not salesperson. It signals direct access and that you care.
- Ask for **two minutes**, not "a moment" — it's specific and respectful of their time.
- **Don't pitch yet.** Just get permission to show.

---

### Discovery — Ask before you tell

Before showing anything, ask two or three questions. This makes the demo personal.

**Best discovery questions:**
- *"Right now, how do your clients book appointments — do they call, walk in, or text you?"*
- *"How often do you get no-shows? Does it happen a lot?"*
- *"When you're in the middle of a cut and your phone rings, do you answer it?"*
- *"Are you on any booking software right now, or just keeping track yourself?"*

Listen. Whatever pain they mention — that's what you lead with in the demo.

---

### The Demo — Show, don't tell

Pull out your phone. Open a live demo shop (set up `demo.barberqueue.pro` or use your test shop).

**Demo flow (under 3 minutes):**

1. **Client view first** — open the booking page. Say:
   > *"This is what your clients see. They pick a service, pick a time, and book — no phone call, no text, no waiting for you to reply. Works 24/7."*

2. **Show the deposit** — say:
   > *"And if you want, they pay a deposit when they book. So if they don't show up — that money is already yours."*

3. **Show the dashboard** — flip to the owner view. Say:
   > *"This is your dashboard. All your appointments for today, who's coming in, what service, how much. You can complete an appointment, mark a no-show, everything in one place."*

4. **Show the AI auto-reply** — mention it casually:
   > *"And when a client texts you at 10pm asking if you have availability Saturday — the AI replies for you automatically with your hours and your booking link. You don't touch your phone."*

5. **Close the demo** — say:
   > *"The whole thing lives at your own link. Something like `kingcuts.barberqueue.pro`. Your link, your brand. You put it on your Instagram bio, your Google Maps profile, your WhatsApp status — and clients start booking without you lifting a finger."*

---

### The Price Conversation

Only bring up price after the demo. Let them ask, or introduce it naturally:

> *"Right now I'm offering a launch rate of $15 a month — flat, no contract, cancel anytime. No percentage of your payments, no hidden fees. Just $15."*

Then stop talking. Let them respond.

If they ask about competitors:
> *"Square charges you 2.6% of every transaction. If you're doing $5,000 a month in card payments, that's $133 going to Square every month — on top of whatever their plan costs. With BarberQueue, $15 flat. We don't touch your money."*

---

### The Close

Don't leave without a clear next step. Either they sign up now, or you schedule a follow-up.

**If they're interested:**
> *"Let's set it up right now — takes 5 minutes. You just need your email and a name for your shop's link."*

Open `barberqueue.pro/register` on their phone or yours. Do it together.

**If they need time:**
> *"Totally fine. Here's my card — I'm on WhatsApp, text me any questions. I'll also send you the link so you can look at it yourself when you have a moment."*

**Never leave empty-handed.** Even a "maybe" gets a card, a WhatsApp contact saved, and a follow-up message that evening.

---

### The Follow-Up (same evening)

Send a WhatsApp message the same day:

> *"Hey [name], great meeting you today at [shop name]. Here's the link to check out BarberQueue: barberqueue.pro. If you want, I can come back and set everything up for you in person — usually takes about 20 minutes and your booking page is live the same day. Let me know!"*

## Common Business Phrases to Know

These are phrases used naturally in B2B conversations in Toronto. Study them so they feel natural.

**Opening & rapport:**
- *"I'll keep it brief, I know you're busy."* — shows respect for their time
- *"I was in the neighborhood and wanted to stop by."* — casual, non-pushy entry
- *"I noticed your shop on Google Maps and wanted to introduce myself."* — specific, shows you did homework
- *"Do you have two minutes? I want to show you something quick."* — specific ask, low commitment

**Creating urgency without pressure:**
- *"I'm only offering the launch rate to the first shops I work with in this area."* — exclusivity
- *"A few shops nearby are already testing it — wanted to give you first look before it spreads."* — social proof
- *"This rate goes up once I get past my first ten clients."* — real and honest

**Handling hesitation:**
- *"I completely understand, no rush."* — disarms defensiveness
- *"What would it take for this to make sense for you?"* — opens dialogue
- *"Fair enough — what's your biggest concern?"* — invites objection so you can address it
- *"No pressure at all — here's my card if you ever want to revisit."* — leaves door open

**Closing:**
- *"Want to give it a shot? First 7 days are on me."* — low risk ask
- *"Let's get you set up right now — it takes five minutes."* — assumes the yes
- *"What do you need to see to feel confident about this?"* — consultative close

**Talking about value:**
- *"Think about what one extra booking a week adds up to over a year."* — quantifies value
- *"The question isn't what it costs — it's what it saves you."* — reframes price
- *"Most of my clients made their money back in the first week."* — ROI framing
- *"This pays for itself with one no-show prevented."* — concrete payoff

**Talking about yourself:**
- *"I built this specifically for shops like yours."* — relevance
- *"I'm the founder, so you get direct access to me — not a call center."* — trust
- *"I'm not here to sell you something. I'm here to solve a problem."* — positioning
- *"We're a small team, so every client matters to us."* — personal touch

## FAQ & Objection Handling

### "How much does it cost?"
> *"$15 a month right now — that's our launch rate. No contract, cancel anytime. After the trial period it's $19.99, but you're locked in at $15 as one of our first clients."*

### "I'm not good with technology."
> *"That's exactly why I'm here. I'll set everything up for you in person. Your hours, your services, your booking link — done before I leave. After that, it's as simple as checking your phone in the morning."*

### "My clients don't use apps."
> *"It's not an app — it's a webpage. Any phone, any browser. Your client gets a text with the link and books in 30 seconds. No download, no account, nothing."*

### "I already use Square."
> *"Square is great for payments, but think about what you're paying them. 2.6% of every transaction — that's hundreds of dollars a month for a busy shop. BarberQueue is $15 flat and you keep 100% of every payment."*

### "I just take walk-ins, I don't do appointments."
> *"Walk-ins are still your bread and butter — this doesn't change that. But a booking system fills your slow hours. The clients who would've walked by come in because they reserved a slot."*

### "What if I want to cancel?"
> *"No contracts. You cancel anytime, no questions asked. I'd rather earn your business every month than lock you in."*

### "Do I need to set up Stripe?"
> *"Yes, but I'll walk you through it right now — takes about 10 minutes. It's how you receive deposits directly into your bank account. We don't touch the money."*

### "What if something breaks or I have a question?"
> *"You have my personal WhatsApp. Text me and I respond. You're not sending a support ticket to India — you're texting the person who built it."*

### "How is this different from just posting on Instagram?"
> *"Instagram gets you visibility. BarberQueue gets you bookings. The link in your Instagram bio sends people to your booking page — they pick a time, pay a deposit, and they show up. Instagram alone doesn't do that."*

### "I need to think about it."
> *"Of course. Can I ask what specifically you need to think through? Sometimes I can clear it up right now."*
> *(If they still want time):* *"Totally fair. I'll send you a message tonight with the link so you can look at it when you're free. Sound good?"*

### "Is there a free plan?"
> *"There's a 7-day free trial — no card needed, full access. After that it's $15 a month. I'm not going to give it away forever because I need to keep the servers running and keep building features. But the trial gives you enough time to see if it's worth it."*

## The Bottom Line

The goal of the first 5 visits is not revenue — it's **one success story**.

One shop that uses BarberQueue, fills their slow hours, stops losing money to no-shows, and is happy to say so on camera or in a WhatsApp message. That story is worth more than any feature you could build right now.

Offer the first 2–3 shops free setup, personal support, and $15/month. In exchange, ask for honest feedback and a short testimonial. Use that testimonial in every visit after.

> *"I set this up for a shop on Eglinton last week. The owner told me he filled three slots in the first two days that he would have missed otherwise. That's $120 back in his pocket."*

That one sentence closes more deals than any feature list.
